# Privacy Policy for Orbit

**Effective Date:** March 1, 2026

I built Orbit because I wanted a tool to manage my personal relationships without handing over my contacts to a third-party server. Because of this, Orbit's privacy policy is incredibly simple: **Your data is yours, and I do not want it.**

## 1. Data Collection & Storage
Orbit is a "local-first" application. All data you enter into Orbit—including contact names, interaction logs, notes, tags, and artifacts—is stored securely on your device using Apple’s local storage frameworks. 

**I do not collect, transmit, store, or have access to any of the data you enter into the app.** ## 2. iCloud Sync
If you are signed into an Apple ID with iCloud Drive enabled, Orbit uses Apple's CloudKit to sync your database across your personal iOS and macOS devices. 
* This sync happens directly between your device and Apple's servers. 
* Your data is stored in your private iCloud database.
* I (the developer) have absolutely no access to this database.

## 3. Apple Contacts Integration
Orbit asks for permission to access your Apple Contacts. This permission is used **strictly locally** on your device to allow you to easily import names, phone numbers, emails, and birthdays into Orbit. 
* None of your Apple Contacts data is ever sent to me or any third-party servers. 
* The import process happens entirely on your device.

## 4. Analytics and Tracking
Orbit contains **zero** third-party analytics trackers, crash reporters, or advertising frameworks. I do not track how you use the app, who you add to the app, or how often you open it. 

## 5. Data Portability
You are never locked into Orbit. You can export your entire database at any time from the in-app Settings menu in both JSON and CSV formats. 

## 6. Changes to This Policy
Because Orbit does not collect data, it is highly unlikely this core philosophy will ever change. If minor updates are made to this policy to reflect new iOS features, the "Effective Date" at the top of this document will be updated.
